# Candidate Consent FAQ

## Purpose

This FAQ gives consultants and support staff consistent candidate-facing
answers for opportunity and consent questions. It is an operational guide, not
a substitute for customer legal review of final consent wording.

## What is this system?

It is a recruiting transaction workflow used by a consultant to manage a
specific opportunity. The system helps organize your profile, documents,
opportunity details, follow-up answers, consent choices, and status. AI may
help summarize or draft information, but humans and system gates control what
becomes confirmed and what is shared.

## What does AI do with my information?

AI can help parse documents, summarize experience, detect missing information,
suggest follow-up questions, and assist matching. AI output is treated as a
claim, not a confirmed fact. Important profile, opportunity, consent, and
disclosure actions require human review or your explicit confirmation.

## What is shared before I give consent?

Before consent and disclosure, the client should only see anonymous,
client-safe information. That can include broad role family, seniority range,
generalized capabilities, redacted strengths, risks, and match rationale. It
must not include your name, contact details, raw resume, direct profile links,
exact employer details that reveal your identity, or consultant-private notes.

## What is L3 consented detail?

L3 means you have consented to share more specific opportunity-related profile
detail for a specific job and profile version. L3 is still not full identity
disclosure. It does not by itself allow the client to see your name or contact
details.

## What is L4 identity disclosure?

L4 means identity or contact information is disclosed after required gates pass.
L4 requires valid consent, a compatible opportunity/profile version, consultant
approval, privacy review, and the system disclosure record. The client should
not receive your identity just because they have a client account or because
they ask informally.

## Is consent reusable for every job?

No. Consent should be tied to a specific opportunity, profile version, consent
text version, and shared-fields preview. If the opportunity, profile version,
or shared fields change materially, the consultant should request updated
confirmation instead of reusing old consent without review.

## Can I decline or revoke consent?

You can decline a request. Revocation handling depends on the current product
workflow and customer policy, but a revoked or expired consent must not be used
as if it were active consent for future disclosure. Ask the consultant to
confirm how the pilot handles already-completed disclosures and future sharing.

## Can I see what will be shared?

The consent request should show or describe the opportunity, profile version,
consent text version, and shared fields. If the shared field preview is unclear,
ask the consultant to clarify before confirming.

## Can I correct my profile?

Yes. Corrections should go through the profile review or follow-up workflow.
Candidate answers and corrections may require consultant confirmation before
they become confirmed profile facts.

## Will the client see my raw resume?

Not before the correct consent and disclosure level. Pre-disclosure client
review should use redacted, client-safe candidate cards. Raw documents and raw
source text are not client-safe by default.

## Will my current employer or project names be visible?

Exact employer, team, project, product, chip, patent, paper, public talk, or
precise timeline details can identify you. Those details should be generalized,
removed, or gated until the appropriate consent and disclosure state is reached.

## Can the consultant share my contact details outside the system?

The operating rule is that identity and contact disclosure should follow the
approved consent and disclosure path. If a manual channel is used during a
controlled pilot, it still needs the same approval and audit discipline.

## What should I do if something looks wrong?

Tell the consultant which field is wrong, what the correct value is, and which
source supports the correction. Do not send unrelated sensitive information.
The consultant should route the correction through the review workflow.

## What should I do if I receive a suspicious or unexpected request?

Do not confirm the request. Contact the named consultant or customer support
path from the pilot onboarding material. Unexpected consent, unlock, payment,
or account requests should be treated as security-sensitive.
