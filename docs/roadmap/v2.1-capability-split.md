# v2.1 Capability Split

## Purpose

This document walks through every v2.1 spec capability area and classifies
each into exactly one of four categories:

- **Complete**: Fully built with persistence, service enforcement, API, tests,
  and audit. Ready for pilot use.
- **Partial**: Backend kernel or contract exists but missing API, UI, real
  execution, or full workflow. State exactly what exists and what is missing.
- **Missing**: Not yet implemented at all. State what needs to be built.
- **Forbidden-to-fake**: Capabilities that must never be faked, stubbed, or
  demo-shortcutted even under time pressure. State the safety reason.

Cross-references use the productization roadmap task numbers (Task 15-60) and
the production kernel roadmap (Task 0-14).

## Source of Truth

- v2.1 (`docs/specs/v2.1/product-spec-v2.1.md`) is the authoritative
  capability list.
- v2.0 (`docs/specs/v2.0/product-spec-v2.0.md`) is the preserved UI baseline.
- `docs/roadmap/implementation-status.md`, `docs/roadmap/known-gaps.md`,
  `docs/roadmap/production-kernel-roadmap.md`, and
  `docs/roadmap/product-scope-after-kernel.md` provide the evidence of what
  Task 0-14 actually built.

## 14. Hard Invariants

These invariants must hold across all current and future tasks:

1. **Backend owns truth.** PostgreSQL is the source of truth. AI orchestration
   platforms, low-code tools, MCP, and LLM vendors must not become the source
   of truth for confirmed facts, consent, disclosure, placement, or commission.

2. **AI outputs claims, not facts.** AI extraction output enters the Claim
   Ledger. Only after risk-tiered human review and CanonicalWriteGate approval
   can claims become canonical facts.

3. **Raw input is not fact.** Uploaded materials, candidate self-statements,
   consultant notes, and AI inferences do not directly equal facts. They must
   preserve source, trust, and status metadata.

4. **Client cannot read raw Candidate before unlock/disclosure.** Before
   identity disclosure, the client sees only ClientSafeCandidateCard through
   the client-safe projection boundary. Raw Candidate and raw CandidateProfile
   are denied to Client at all service and API boundaries.

5. **L3 consented detail is not L4 identity disclosure.** Sharing more
   detailed information under L3 does not grant identity disclosure. L4
   requires confirmed non-expired/non-revoked consent, approved human unlock
   decision, approved disclosure record, and an explicit
   DISCLOSURE_IDENTITY_DISCLOSED WorkflowEvent.

6. **Bulk approve cannot produce CANDIDATE_CONFIRMED or EXTERNAL_VERIFIED.**
   The maximum status from bulk approve is HUMAN_ACKNOWLEDGED. Higher
   verification requires individual candidate confirmation or external evidence.

7. **CanonicalWriteGate must not be bypassed.** Every canonical fact write
   must pass through the gate. Admin, System, and AI roles cannot bypass the
   gate by role alone.

8. **Every key state transition must create WorkflowEvent.** Consent,
   disclosure, canonical write, placement, commission, and workflow transitions
   must be auditably recorded with actor, entity, action, before/after state,
   reason, timestamp, and AI involvement.

9. **AI cannot disclose identity, unlock contact information, make commercial
   promises, reject candidates, or overwrite human-confirmed facts.**

10. **WorkflowEventService and WorkflowTransitionAuditService are audit
    boundaries, not workflow engines.** They record transitions; they do not
    validate transition legality or mutate entity state.
